# 05. Use Cases

## 5.1 Dynamic pricing and intelligent procurement

Scenario:

- User agents request multi-attribute offers across merchants.
- Merchant agents issue adaptive quotes based on inventory, demand, and customer value.

Game-theoretic insight:

- If search is cheap and switching is frictionless, merchants face tighter equilibrium spreads.
- Mechanism design can reward truthful availability and penalize deceptive quote inflation.

Business impact:

- higher conversion quality,
- lower customer acquisition waste,
- faster quote-to-order cycles.

## 5.2 Autonomous checkout and payment routing

Scenario:

- User agent selects checkout terms,
- Bank agent routes payment across rails to optimize finality, cost, and risk,
- Merchant agent accepts based on net expected margin.

Optimization goal:

    maximize expected transaction utility subject to success-probability and compliance constraints.

Business impact:

- improved authorization success,
- reduced payment leakage,
- lower blended payment cost.

## 5.3 Cross-border tokenized settlement

Scenario:

- Merchant sells cross-border,
- Bank/payment agents choose between correspondent banking and tokenized rails,
- User agent values certainty and speed.

Mechanism insight:

- programmable escrow and atomic delivery-vs-payment reduce settlement risk,
- explicit penalty and fallback rules reduce dispute externalities.

Business impact:

- faster settlement finality,
- lower reconciliation burden,
- improved treasury predictability.

## 5.4 Programmable B2B procurement networks

Scenario:

- Enterprise buyer agents negotiate volume tiers and delivery windows,
- Supplier agents optimize fill rate and margin,
- Finance agents provide dynamic credit terms.

Model implication:

- repeated-game reputation and contract compliance scores support cooperative equilibria,
- strategic defaults become less attractive when future access is reputation-gated.

Business impact:

- improved procurement resilience,
- lower stockout risk,
- better working-capital efficiency.

## 5.5 Regulated digital-asset commerce

Scenario:

- Tokenized assets are used for settlement or collateral,
- Compliance constraints vary by jurisdiction and product class.

Governance insight:

- machine-verifiable policy checks and auditable logs are required for scalable regulator confidence.

Business impact:

- safer innovation pathways,
- lower enforcement risk,
- faster approval cycles for new products.
