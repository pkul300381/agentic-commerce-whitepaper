# References

## Foundational game theory

- Nash, J. F. (1950). Equilibrium points in n-person games. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 36(1), 48-49.
- von Neumann, J., & Morgenstern, O. (1944). Theory of Games and Economic Behavior. Princeton University Press.
- Fudenberg, D., & Tirole, J. (1991). Game Theory. MIT Press.
- Osborne, M. J., & Rubinstein, A. (1994). A Course in Game Theory. MIT Press.

## Mechanism design and market design

- Hurwicz, L. (1973). The design of mechanisms for resource allocation. American Economic Review, 63(2), 1-30.
- Myerson, R. B. (1981). Optimal auction design. Mathematics of Operations Research, 6(1), 58-73.
- Roth, A. E. (2008). What have we learned from market design? Economic Journal, 118(527), 285-310.
- Milgrom, P. (2004). Putting Auction Theory to Work. Cambridge University Press.

## Multi-agent systems and AI economics

- Wooldridge, M. (2009). An Introduction to MultiAgent Systems (2nd ed.). Wiley.
- Shoham, Y., & Leyton-Brown, K. (2009). Multiagent Systems: Algorithmic, Game-Theoretic, and Logical Foundations. Cambridge University Press.
- Parkes, D. C., & Wellman, M. P. (2015). Economic reasoning and artificial intelligence. Science, 349(6245), 267-272.
- Varian, H. R. (2019). Artificial intelligence, economics, and industrial organization. In The Economics of Artificial Intelligence.

## Digital payments, fintech, and governance context

- BIS Committee on Payments and Market Infrastructures. (2023). Fast payments and data-rich payments.
- Financial Stability Board. (2023). Regulation, supervision and oversight of crypto-asset activities and markets.
- IOSCO. (2023). Policy recommendations for crypto and digital asset markets.
- OECD. (2024). AI governance and accountability in high-impact sectors.
