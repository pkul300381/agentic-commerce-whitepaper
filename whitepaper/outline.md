# Whitepaper Outline

## Title

A Mathematical Model for Multi-Agent AI Systems Using Game Theory

## Positioning

A strategic and technical framework for understanding how autonomous AI agents negotiate, transact, and optimize value in agentic commerce.

## Audience

- Fintech leaders
- AI companies
- Regulators
- CTOs

## Tone

Visionary + technical

## Structure

1. Introduction: why agentic commerce now, and what problem the paper solves.
2. Agentic Commerce: participants, protocol primitives, and transaction lifecycle.
3. Game-Theory Model: repeated non-cooperative interactions and equilibrium behavior.
4. Mathematical Framework: objective functions, constraints, and mechanism tuning.
5. Use Cases: dynamic pricing, payment routing, cross-border settlement, B2B procurement.
6. Risks and Governance: manipulation risk, policy-as-code, monitoring, and stress testing.
7. Conclusion: implementation roadmap and strategic implications.

## Core mathematical tools

- Non-cooperative game theory
- Nash equilibrium analysis
- Mechanism design
- Multi-agent optimization

## Output objective

Provide a deployable conceptual model that helps organizations design AI-native commercial protocols that are efficient, compliant, and resilient.
